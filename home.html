Wings of fire

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Wings of Fire" is an inspiring autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, India's former President and one of its most renowned scientists. The book chronicles his journey from a humble background in Rameswaram to becoming a key figure in India's space and missile programs. It highlights his early life, education, struggle, and major contributions to ISRO and DRDO. The book is a motivational read, especially for students and young dreamers, showcasing the power of determination, hard work, and faith in one’s dreams.

Whispers of Divine Light: Unlocking the Secrets of Faith and Joy

Dr. Hari Chinthakunta

Whispers of Divine Light is a spiritual compilation featuring profound dialogues between Bhagwan Sri Ram “SIR” and the late Dr. Nagaraj, revered as Sadhguru Paramahamsa Naga Yogiraj. These exchanges, drawn from his diaries and letters, are portrayed as rays of divine illumination offering deep wisdom and guidance, particularly as Dr. Nagaraj passed away on August 3, 2022. The core messages revolve around transcendental illumination, divine love, and spiritual awakening, aiming to guide readers toward renewed faith, inner peace, and purpose amid life’s trials

Harry Potter

J.K. Rowling

Harry Potter is a bestselling fantasy book series written by J.K. Rowling, consisting of seven novels (1997–2007). It follows the journey of Harry Potter, an orphan who discovers on his 11th birthday that he is a wizard. He attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he learns magic, makes friends (Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley), and uncovers the truth about his parents’ death. Harry becomes embroiled in the fight against the dark wizard Lord Voldemort, who seeks immortality and domination over both the magical and non-magical worlds. The series blends themes of friendship, bravery, love, and the battle between good and evil.

It ends with us

Colleen Hoover

It Ends with Us is a contemporary romance and drama novel by Colleen Hoover, first published in 2016. It tells the emotional story of Lily Bloom, a young woman who starts a new life in Boston and falls in love with a charming neurosurgeon, Ryle Kincaid. As their relationship grows, Lily discovers Ryle’s darker side, forcing her to confront difficult choices about love, abuse, and self-worth. The novel also explores Lily’s past through diary entries about her first love, Atlas Corrigan, who unexpectedly reappears in her life.

Mahabharata

Sage Vyasa

The Mahabharata is one of the greatest epics of ancient India, composed in Sanskrit and considered the longest poem in the world. It narrates the story of the Kurukshetra War between the Pandavas and Kauravas, two branches of the Kuru dynasty, and explores complex themes of duty (dharma), righteousness, justice, and human nature. Alongside its central war narrative, it contains numerous sub-stories, moral teachings, and philosophical discourses, including the revered Bhagavad Gita, which is a dialogue between Lord Krishna and Arjuna about life, duty, and spirituality. The epic is not only a historical and mythological treasure but also a guide to ethics, governance, and the consequences of human actions.

My Success Story

B.V. Pattabhiram

My Success Story is an inspiring autobiographical account by B.V. Pattabhiram, a well-known psychologist, hypnotherapist, and motivational speaker. Ramayana

Sage Valmiki

The Ramayana is one of the two great epics of ancient India, composed in Sanskrit, and tells the story of Lord Rama, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It follows Rama’s life—from his birth in Ayodhya to his exile in the forest, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon king Ravana, and the eventual battle in Lanka to rescue her, aided by his loyal brother Lakshmana and devotee Hanuman. Beyond being a tale of heroism and adventure, the Ramayana is a profound moral and spiritual guide, emphasizing virtues like truth, duty (dharma), devotion, loyalty, and the triumph of good over evil. It has been retold in many languages and forms across India and Southeast Asia, influencing culture, art, and values for centuries.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Robert T. Kiyosaki

Rich Dad Poor Dad is a personal finance classic by Robert T. Kiyosaki that contrasts the money mindset and financial habits of two father figures in the author’s life—his own highly educated but financially struggling “poor dad” and his best friend’s wealthy, self-made “rich dad.” Through stories and lessons from both men, Kiyosaki explains how financial success depends more on financial literacy, investing, and building assets than on formal education or high income alone. The book challenges traditional beliefs about money, encourages entrepreneurial thinking, and teaches principles like making money work for you, understanding cash flow, and creating multiple streams of income. Written in a simple, conversational style, it inspires readers to take control of their financial future.

Romeo and Juliet

William Shakespeare

Romeo and Juliet is a timeless tragedy by William Shakespeare, first performed in the late 16th century. Set in Verona, Italy, it tells the story of two young lovers—Romeo Montague and Juliet Capulet—whose families are embroiled in a bitter feud. Despite their families’ hatred, Romeo and Juliet fall deeply in love and secretly marry, hoping their union will bring peace. However, a series of misunderstandings and ill-fated events leads to their untimely deaths, which finally reconciles their families. The play explores universal themes of love, fate, conflict, and the consequences of prejudice, making it one of the most enduring works in literature and theater.

Spider-Man

Stan Lee and Steve Ditko

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero appearing in Marvel Comics, first introduced in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962. The character, Peter Parker, is a high school student from New York who gains superhuman abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. He develops enhanced strength, agility, the ability to cling to walls, and a “spider-sense” that warns him of danger. Balancing his personal life, education, and relationships while fighting crime, Peter adopts the guiding principle taught by his Uncle Ben: “With great power comes great responsibility.” Over the decades, Spider-Man has faced a wide array of iconic villains, such as Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, and Venom, and has appeared in comics, animated series, blockbuster films, and video games, becoming one of the most beloved superheroes worldwide.

The God of Small Things

Arundhati Roy

The God of Small Things is a Booker Prize–winning novel by Arundhati Roy, first published in 1997. Set in Kerala, India, it tells the story of fraternal twins Estha and Rahel, whose lives are shaped by the events of their childhood and the rigid social and cultural rules of their community. Moving fluidly between past and present, the novel explores themes of love, loss, family, caste discrimination, and forbidden relationships. The narrative style is poetic and non-linear, capturing moments both ordinary and tragic with vivid detail. Through its portrayal of “small things” that impact human lives, the book offers a deeply emotional and thought-provoking commentary on society and the personal cost of defying its norms.

The Magic of the Lost Temple

Sudha Murty

The Magic of the Lost Temple is a heartwarming children’s novel by Sudha Murty, set against the charming backdrop of rural Karnataka. It centers on Nooni, a city girl who spends her summer vacation in her grandparents' village, Somanahalli, where she gradually embraces the slower pace of village life—joining in papad-making, picnics, and learning to ride a bicycle. Adventure awaits when she discovers a hidden, ancient stepwell deep in the forest, steeped in local history and mystery. Sudha Murty’s simple yet evocative storytelling beautifully weaves together themes of cultural heritage, curiosity, friendship, and family bonds, inviting young readers to both explore and cherish the richness of their roots.

The Psychology of Money

Morgan Housel

The Psychology of Money: Timeless Lessons on Wealth, Greed, and Happiness by Morgan Housel is a bestselling personal finance book that focuses on the human side of money management. Instead of giving complex investment formulas, it explores how attitudes, emotions, and behaviors influence financial decisions more than technical knowledge. Through 19 engaging short stories, Housel illustrates key lessons on topics like the power of compounding, the role of luck and risk, and the importance of long-term thinking. The book emphasizes that building wealth is less about intelligence and more about discipline, patience, and understanding one’s own relationship with money

The Secret Garden

Frances Hodgson Burnett

The Secret Garden is a classic children’s novel by Frances Hodgson Burnett, first published in 1911. It tells the story of Mary Lennox, a spoiled and lonely girl sent to live with her reclusive uncle in a large, gloomy mansion on the Yorkshire moors after her parents die. While exploring the estate, Mary discovers a hidden, neglected garden locked away for years. With the help of new friends—Dickon, a nature-loving boy, and her sickly cousin Colin—she works to restore the garden, and in the process, all three children undergo emotional and physical transformations. The novel celebrates the healing power of nature, friendship, and kindness, showing how love and care can bring both people and places back to life.

The Secret

Rhonda Byrne

The Secret is a self-help book by Rhonda Byrne, first published in 2006, that popularizes the concept of the Law of Attraction—the idea that thoughts can directly influence reality. Drawing on quotes and teachings from historical figures, spiritual leaders, and modern-day experts, the book encourages readers to focus on positive thinking, visualization, and gratitude to attract success, health, wealth, and happiness into their lives. Byrne explains that by aligning your mindset with your desires and believing they are already yours, you can manifest them into reality. Written in an accessible, inspirational style, The Secret has become a global phenomenon, inspiring millions to adopt a more optimistic and intentional approach to life.

Sudha Murty

Grandma’s Bag of Stories is a delightful children’s book by Sudha Murty, filled with warm, engaging tales that reflect Indian culture, values, and everyday wisdom. Set in the quiet town of Shiggaon, the book follows a group of children who gather around their grandmother, Ajji, during the summer holidays. Written in Sudha Murty’s simple yet captivating style, the stories are easy to understand, making them perfect for bedtime reading or classroom sharing, while subtly instilling timeless values in young readers.

Atomic Habits

James Clear

Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones by James Clear is a bestselling self-improvement book that explains how small, consistent changes can lead to remarkable results over time. Clear presents a practical framework for habit formation based on four steps—cue, craving, response, and reward—and shows how to use the Four Laws of Behavior Change to make good habits inevitable and bad habits difficult. Through real-life examples, psychological insights, and actionable strategies, the book teaches readers how to design their environment, track progress, and stay motivated. Emphasizing that habits are the compound interest of self-improvement, it inspires readers to focus on tiny daily actions that accumulate into lasting success.
